About me

Hi! I'm Chloe Hafner, a product and user experience designer building empathetic and inclusive menopause care experiences at Gennev in Seattle.

Why I love UX

I began to realize that I had been doing UX design long before I learned it was a possible career path. I’ve always found myself drawn to solving problems creatively, connecting with others on a deeper level, and creating experiences that spark joy, comfortability, and empathy, whether that’s by helping small businesses and web developers improve their websites, trying a new recipe, or ensuring that diverse voices and perspectives are respected, valued, and amplified before moving forward with a solution.

I enrolled in the Google UX Design Professional Certificate Program, immersing myself in the world of UX Design, expanding my toolkit and amplifying my passion for the field.

My past experiences

Before pivoting into UX Design, I pursued tech sales. While I enjoyed my work, I wasn’t fulfilled. I wanted to use more of my creativity, empathy, and design skills in my job. I began researching possible career paths and discovered UX design. It felt like I had met my perfect match: a career where I could combine my passions, interests, and talents to make a meaningful impact on others.
